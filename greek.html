The Greek Alphabet

The Greek Cases

Greek nouns change their endings according to grammatical function. These forms are called cases.

Case Function Example
Nominative Subject ὁ λόγος
Genitive Possession / Source τοῦ λόγου
Dative Indirect Object / Means τῷ λόγῳ
Accusative Direct Object τὸν λόγον
ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος
“In the beginning was the Word.” — John 1:1
Important: The genitive case may express possession, description, relationship, source, separation, comparison, apposition, and many other functions.

Present Active Indicative

The Greek present tense usually expresses continuous or repeated action.

  • ο = connecting vowel

The Aorist Tense

The aorist usually presents action as a complete whole. Many grammarians interpret it primarily in terms of aspect.

First Aorist Active

Person Singular Plural
1stἔλυσαἐλύσαμεν
2ndἔλυσαςἐλύσατε
3rdἔλυσε(ν)ἔλυσαν
Augment: Past indicative verbs commonly use an augment. In ἔλυσα, epsilon is prefixed to the verbal stem.
