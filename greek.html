Breathing Marks

Greek words beginning with vowels or rho take breathing marks. The smooth breathing indicates absence of aspiration, while the rough breathing indicates an initial h sound.

Mark Name Example
᾿ Smooth Breathing ἀνήρ
῾ Rough Breathing ἁμαρτία

The Greek Cases

Greek nouns change their endings according to grammatical function.

Case Function Example
Nominative Subject ὁ λόγος
Genitive Possession / Source τοῦ λόγου
Dative Indirect Object / Means τῷ λόγῳ
Accusative Direct Object τὸν λόγον
ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος
“In the beginning was the Word.” — John 1:1

The Definite Article

The Greek article is highly inflected and agrees with its noun in gender, number, and case.

Case Masculine Feminine Neuter
Nominative Singular ὁ ἡ τό
Genitive Singular τοῦ τῆς τοῦ
Dative Singular τῷ τῇ τῷ
Accusative Singular τόν τήν τό
