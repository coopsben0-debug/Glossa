Critical Rules for Diphthongs & Breathing:
When two vowels blend into one syllable, they form a diphthong (e.g., αι, ει, οι, αυ, ου, υι). Every word starting with a vowel requires a breathing mark:
  • Rough Breathing ( ̔ ): Adds an "h" sound. Example: ὑπέρ (hu-per).
  • Smooth Breathing ( ̓ ): Dictates zero sound alteration. Example: ἀκούω (a-koo-o).

The Third Declension (Consonant Stems)

Third declension structures feature shifting consonant stems. Case suffix allocations often cause spelling changes when coming into contact with the case endings.

Consonant Stem Shift Rules

  • Labials (β, π, φ) + ς $\rightarrow$ ψ
  • Palatals (γ, κ, χ) + ς $\rightarrow$ ξ
  • Dentals (δ, τ, θ) + ς $\rightarrow$ drop out entirely

Verbal paradigms define continuous or active linear processes taking place in the present timeline. Suffix combinations explicitly register person and count constraints.

The Movable Nu ($\nu$):
The terminal variable ν inside the 3rd person plural suffix (-ουσιν) acts as a structural phonetic buffer. It is dynamically appended when the succeeding word begins with a vowel or occurs at the end of a sentence block.

Future Tense & Contract Rules

The standard future active system is formed by inserting a tense suffix token (σ) between the root stem and the standard personal variable endings.

Contract Vowel Rules

When short vowel stems (α, ε, ο) meet standard personal endings, they undergo systematic contractions:

  • ε + ω $\rightarrow$ ω (e.g., ποιέω $\rightarrow$ ποιῶ)
  • ε + ε $\rightarrow$ ει (e.g., ποιέετε $\rightarrow$ ποιεῖτε)
  • ε + ο $\rightarrow$ ου (e.g., ποιέομεν $\rightarrow$ ποιοῦμεν)

First and Second Aorist Systems

The Aorist Tense denotes past, punctiliar actions. It is characterized by an initial **past augment prefix** (ἐ-) added to the front of the verb stem.

First Aorist vs. Second Aorist Morphologies

  • First Aorist (Weak): Uses a structural sigma-alpha (-σα) suffix modifier.
  • Second Aorist (Strong): Changes the underlying spelling of the word root instead of adding a suffix token.
